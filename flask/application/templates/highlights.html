Project Highlights

The project is still a work in progress! Here we share some highlights of our visualizations and analyses so far.

Monthly Average

Plots showing averaged monthly totals for thefts and robberies, respectively. The envelope shows the standard deviation.

Thefts and robberies appear to occur at a higher rate during summer months than winter months.

Time of Day Distribution

Histograms showing respectively theft incidents and robbery incidents binned by hour of the day. Also plotted are probability density functions estimated using gaussian kernel density estimation. The so-called Silverman's rule for optimal bandwidth choice is used.

Thefts are most frequent in the afternoon and evening, with a smaller second peak at late hours of the night. Their least frequent time is the early hours of the morning.

In contrast, robberies are at their least frequent in the late morning and at their most frequent in the late hours of the night.

Daily High Temperature

Bar plots of average daily thefts and robberies, respectively, against the daily high temperature.

Spatial Distribution

Contour plots of spatial probability density functions from kernel density estimates for thefts and robberies, respectively. Red means that a higher probability that a theft or robbery incident would be at that geographic location. Kernel density estimates were generated with Gaussian kernels and a bandwidth chosen by Silverman's Rule.
